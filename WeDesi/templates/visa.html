H-1B Visa

The H-1B visa allows U.S. employers to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations that require theoretical and practical application of a body of highly specialized knowledge. This visa is commonly used by technology companies, universities, and medical facilities to bring in skilled professionals.

Application Steps:

  1. Employer Petition: The U.S. employer files a petition with USCIS.
  2. Labor Condition Application (LCA): The employer must obtain an approved LCA from the Department of Labor.
  3. Form I-129: The employer submits Form I-129 (Petition for a Nonimmigrant Worker) to USCIS, including the approved LCA and other required documentation.
  4. Wait for Approval: If the petition is approved, the employee can apply for an H-1B visa at a U.S. consulate.
  5. Visa Issuance: Upon approval, the employee receives their visa and can begin working in the U.S.

F-1 Visa

The F-1 visa is designed for international students who wish to study in the United States at an accredited institution. This visa is non-immigrant and is meant for those who intend to return to their home country upon completion of their studies. F-1 students are allowed to work on campus and may be eligible for Optional Practical Training (OPT) to gain practical experience in their field of study.

Application Steps:

  1. Acceptance by a SEVP School: Obtain an acceptance letter from a U.S. school certified by the Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP).
  2. Form I-20: The school will issue Form I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status).
  3. SEVIS Fee: Pay the SEVIS fee.
  4. Form DS-160: Complete the DS-160 (Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application) and schedule a visa interview.
  5. Visa Interview: Attend the visa interview at a U.S. consulate or embassy.
  6. Visa Issuance: If approved, the visa will be issued, allowing the student to enter the U.S.

F-1 OPT (Optional Practical Training)

Optional Practical Training (OPT) allows F-1 visa holders to work in their field of study for up to 12 months after completing their academic program. OPT is intended to provide students with hands-on experience and enhance their education. STEM graduates may be eligible for a 24-month extension, making it a total of 36 months for certain fields.

Application Steps:

  1. Apply for OPT: File Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization) with USCIS, typically during the last semester of study.
  2. Receive EAD: Upon approval, receive an Employment Authorization Document (EAD).
  3. Find Employment: Begin working in a role related to your field of study.

F-1 STEM OPT (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)

The STEM OPT extension allows F-1 students who have completed a degree in a STEM field to extend their OPT period by an additional 24 months. This extension is designed to help U.S. employers retain skilled workers in critical fields and provides additional time for international students to gain practical experience.

Application Steps:

  1. Eligibility Check: Ensure your degree is in a STEM field and the employer is enrolled in E-Verify.
  2. Apply for STEM OPT: File Form I-765 with USCIS for the STEM OPT extension.
  3. Receive EAD: Upon approval, receive a new EAD valid for the extended period.
  4. Ongoing Reporting: Submit regular reports to your Designated School Official (DSO) and comply with program requirements.
